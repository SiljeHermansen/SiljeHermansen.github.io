Who am I?

Welcome to my website! I currently work at PluriCourts, a centre of excellence dedicated to the study of international courts at the University of Oslo.

My research focuses on the selection and behavior of our representatives; be they legislators or judges. I am particularly interested in the EU institutions: The European Parliament and the Court of Justice of the European Union. I rely mostly on quantitative methods, and have extensive experience in both automated data collection and Bayesian estimation strategies.

Much of my teaching has been done on the topics of comparative politics and research methods. I’ve tought courses both at bachelor’s and master’s level, for students as well as professionals. My book, Lær deg R (2019) is the first introduction to R in Norwegian.

All CJEU EP

Decision makers in the Court of Justice of the European Union (CJEU)

I open the black box of the decision-making process to consider governments’ and judges’ strategic behavior in the Court of Justice of the European Union (CJEU).

Decision makers in the European Parliament (EP)

Members of the European Parliament obtain influence from their parliamentary groups, but hold their office because of choices made by voters and national parties. This project delves into the dynamic between behavior in Parliament and electoral incentives.
